Background

Who I Am

I am a PhD candidate at the Joint Quantum Institute, University of Maryland, working under the supervision of Prof. Mohammad Hafezi. My research lies at the intersection of quantum optics, nanophotonics, and condensed matter physics.

Previously, I completed my MASc in Electrical and Computer Engineering (Quantum Information) at the Institute for Quantum Computing, University of Waterloo, where I worked with Prof. Michal Bajcsy on creating and shaping light at the single-photon level. Before that, I completed my undergraduate in Electronics and Telecommunication Engineering from Jadavpur University, India.

Research Focus

2023 – PresentPhD
Topological Photonics for Nonlinear Interactions & Photonic Computing
Joint Quantum Institute, UMD
Designing photonic integrated circuits (silicon nitride, lithium niobate, III-V materials) using analytical techniques and commercial simulation tools for engineering optical nonlinearity for frequency comb generation and photonic computing. Worked with foundries for performing tape-out and characterization of photonic integrated circuits.
Science (2026) → See Patents →
2021 – PresentPhD
Optically Probing Strongly Correlated Electronic States in 2D Semiconductors
Joint Quantum Institute, UMD
Using quantum optics and light-matter interaction tools to explore many-body physics, topology, and chirality in 2D semiconductors. Designed, built, and maintained optical setups for real-/momentum-space imaging, ultrafast spectroscopy, nonlinear optics, interferometry, and time-tagged measurements in two 4K cryostats and one dilution fridge.

2019 – 2021MASc
Deterministic Single Photon Subtraction for Engineering Exotic Non-Classical States of Light
IQC, University of Waterloo
Analytical and numerical study proposing deterministic single photon subtraction from arbitrary states using a single quantum emitter coupled to a chiral waveguide or cavity. Demonstrated applications in quantum information theory and metrology.

2019 – 2021MASc
Inverse Design of Optical Antennas for Shaping Emission from NV Centers
IQC, University of Waterloo
Used adjoint optimization and inverse design to engineer diamond nanophotonic devices enabling highly directional emission from nitrogen-vacancy centers.
